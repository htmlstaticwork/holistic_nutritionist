The Role of the Nervous System in Flare-Ups.

April 15, 2026 7 Min Read By Dr. Elena Aura

For years, the medical community viewed stress as a secondary factor in autoimmune disease. However, emerging research in psychoneuroimmunology shows that the nervous system and the immune system are effectively two parts of the same conversation.

1. The Sympathetic Surge

When we are in a chronic 'fight or flight' state, our body produces high levels of cortisol. While cortisol is anti-inflammatory in short bursts, chronic elevation leads to 'glucocorticoid resistance,' where the immune system stops responding to the signal and inflammation runs rampant.

2. Vagus Nerve Tone

The Vagus nerve is the command center for 'Rest and Digest.' High vagal tone acts as a brake on the immune system, preventing it from overreacting to environment triggers. Strengthening this tone is physical work, not just mental.

"Your immune system is listening to your thoughts and your physiological state every second of the day."

3. Practical Regulation

Breathwork and grounding are not just 'wellness topics'—they are biological interventions that shift the pH of the blood and send a signal of safety to every cell in the body.

Conclusion

Healing cannot happen in a state of high alert. By prioritizing your nervous system alongside your nutrition, you address the root frequency of your inflammation, allowing your body the space it needs to repair.
